Childhood Years

I am Heinrich Von Solpico, 20 years old, from Laguna. I am the firstborn child of two. Growing up, I had many interests as a kid. Back then, I was fascinated by construction toys, cars, basketball, and many other things. During my childhood, I learned how to use a computer, and it amazed me how I could play games and explore different things on it. I spent hours discovering new programs and figuring out how technology worked. I studied elementary at SSACLC, where I met my friends and truly enjoyed my school life. I loved learning new things, and school became a place where I discovered my passions. My teachers were kind and helped me improve my skills. After school, I enjoyed playing outside with my friends. We would ride bikes, play basketball, and sometimes just run around for fun. Those moments made my childhood exciting and memorable. However, despite my happy childhood, I was prone to getting sick easily. Because of this, my parents were very protective of me. They forbade me from staying outside too late, especially at night when the cold wind could make me ill. They were also very strict about the food I ate because my stomach would get upset easily. I also remember my parents and grandmother frequently scolding me because of my "kalokohan" or mischievousness. Sometimes, I got into trouble for being too playful or curious. Even with these challenges, I still had a wonderful childhood. I spent time with my family, who always took care of me. I enjoyed the simple joys of being a kid, whether it was playing, learning, or spending time at home. Looking back, my childhood was a mix of fun, learning, and challenges. It shaped me into the person I am today, and I cherish all the memories I made along the way.

Teenage Years

In my teenage years, I studied junior high school at SMAN and senior high school at SPC. As I grew older, my interests started to narrow down, focusing mainly on basketball and cars. Basketball became more than just a hobby; it gave me a sense of excitement and discipline. I enjoyed competing with my friends and constantly working to improve my skills. High school was a memorable time in my life, filled with both fun and challenges. While studying, I met a lot of friends who made my teenage years exciting. We would always hang out together, whether during breaks, weekends, or after school. We shared unforgettable moments, from simple lunch breaks to school events and friendly games of basketball. Exploring new things was part of growing up, and my teenage years were the best phase of my life. I experienced the thrill of late-night conversations, weekend hangouts, and even late-night parties. One of the most unforgettable experiences I had was when I was taken to the police station at 15. I was with my friends during curfew hours, unaware that curfew was actually in place. Since I was the only minor, I was the only one taken while my friends were let go. It was a wake-up call, teaching me the importance of following rules. During this time, I also grew a strong passion for cars. I loved learning about different car models, engines, and modifications. Watching car videos and reading about vehicle performance became a daily routine for me. Eventually, I discovered my love for driving, and every time I got behind the wheel, I felt a sense of freedom and excitement. As part of my exploration, I also tried drinking alcohol and vaping. However, I quickly realized they werenâ€™t for me, and I learned important lessons from those experiences. My worst memory happened post-pandemic when I was 17. I dislocated my knee while playing basketball, which forced me to change how I played. The injury was painful and took months of recovery, making me more cautious whenever I played sports. Despite challenges, my teenage years were filled with fun and self-discovery. Looking back, I cherish all the memories, friendships, and lessons I gained.

College Years

In my current college life, my interests have narrowed once again, focusing solely on cars. Due to my high school injury and the thigh surgery I had in May 2024, I can no longer play basketball the way I used to. Because of this, my passion for cars grew even more as I found new excitement in learning about them. My college life at the university has been fun because I met many new friends. However, I am not as social as I was before, as I tend to keep to myself more. Unlike in high school, where I always hung out with a big group, in college, I prefer a smaller circle of friends. I still enjoy spending time with them, but I am now more selective with who I spend my time with. As I transitioned out of my teenage years, I became more mature in my decisions. I stopped some of my bad habits and became more conscious of my lifestyle. The late-night parties that were once frequent in my teenage years have now become less important to me. Instead, I prefer late-night hangouts with close friends, especially when I am back home in the province. These moments feel more meaningful, and I appreciate them more than ever. College has also taught me how to manage my time better. Balancing academics, social life, and personal interests can be challenging, but I have learned how to prioritize what truly matters. My passion for cars has continued to grow, and I spend a lot of time researching, watching videos, and even dreaming about owning my own dream car someday. I have also started appreciating long drives, as they give me a sense of freedom and peace. Unlike high school, college has made me more independent and responsible. I have learned how to handle different challenges without relying too much on others. I have also become more careful with my choices, ensuring they align with my future goals. Even though college can be stressful, I try to enjoy every moment and make the best out of it. The friendships I have made here are special, and I hope they last a lifetime. Looking ahead, I am excited about what the future holds for me. I know that my experiences in college will shape me into a better version of myself.
